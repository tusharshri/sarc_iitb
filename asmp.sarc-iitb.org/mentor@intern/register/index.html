What is Speed Mentoring ?

“Mentoring is a brain to pick, an ear to listen and a push in the right direction”
- John C.Crosby
Alumni Student Mentorship Program (ASMP) was launched in January 2010 as an in-semester program to mend the link between students and alumni. With the aim of helping students who go on summer internships, Student Alumni Relations Cell (SARC) has launched the Mentor@Intern Program in April 2013. An effort will be made to connect the students going for summer internship in a city, to the alumni based in that city. The purpose of this initiative is to assist the students in getting settled with the alumnus acting as a mentor.

This initiative emphasizes on:

1. Providing students a platform for one to one interaction with their esteemed alumni in the closest city

2. Creating a valuable support system for students in the form of Alumni acting as mentors.

3. Rendering students with career guidance.

4. Tailoring them for their future endeavors.

5. Catering to the betterment of alumni-student relationships by engaging them in fruitful conversations.

Rules and Policies

1. The allotment for the “Mentor@Intern” Initiative is done on the discretion of the ASMP team and keeping in mind various factors like geographical proximity, workplace etc. for the duration of the internship. Once done the allotment will be final and the ASMP Team will not entertain requests to change mentors during this duration of the internship.

2. Mentors will be given basic details of the students who have been allotted to him/he. Hence kindly fill all the details with care. Any improper details undermining the credibility of the Programme will result in immediate disqualification.

3. No personal requests/favors will be entertained by ASMP team regarding allotment of any particular mentor to any particular mentee.

4. We expect the Mentee to initiate the interaction, and subsequently remain in contact through any means of communication. At the end of the internship, it is imperative that students and mentors provide feedback when contacted for the same. If an alumnus complains about lack of response the student will be warned and subsequently deregistered.

5. The Mentor should not be asked for any kind of personal benefits like interns/placements for a later period. In case of such an incident, we request the mentor to communicate the same to us.

6. We expect students to understand that alumni relations are very important for the institute so please do not do anything that can jeopardize these relations and take the above rules seriously. Violations of the above rules can result in the de registration of the concerned student.


mentor@intern report | Summer 2013

As part of our continuous efforts to keep the Alumni involved in the growth of our institution and in order to bring together closer both the current students and the alumni, SARC started an initiative known as the Mentor@Intern on the lines of the Alumni Student Mentorship Program. The aim of this program was to help internship students settle down in their new work environment with the help of our esteemed Alumni by their side during every step of their struggle. The program was launched for the first time this year in the month of May,2013. A detailed report of the program has been shared below.

Mentors registered : 40

Mentees registered : 48

Duration of Program : May 15 - End of Summer Break

Pre-Mentor@Intern Activities:

Before the program was officially launched mails were sent on April 17 to all the students regarding Mentor@Intern. Based on the responses that we got, we sent mails to the Alumni giving them a basic idea about the initiative and what was expected of them as a mentor, along with the links for registration. The necessary mentor details that we required for this were obtained from the past mentors database of ASMP. Once a list of mentors and mentees were obtained we started the allotment process which was done manually. The criteria on the basis of which the allotment was done are,

1. City of Internship

2. Department

3. Company of Internship

In most of the cases every mentor was allotted only one mentee. But in those cases where the number of mentors were much lower than the number of mentees multiple mentees were allotted to mentors based on the consent of the mentors. After the allotment was done we sent mails to both the mentors and mentees informing them about the allotment. In these mails we also shared the profiles of both the mentor and mentee with each other and encouraged them to start interactions at the earliest.Another important effort done by us in order to give both the mentees and the mentors a complete idea about the program - A Handbook,  prepared by our team :

http://asmp.sarc-iitb.org/mentor@intern/MenteeBrochureFinal.pdf

Activity done during Mentor@Intern:

Once the allotment was done and the mails were sent we called the mentees during the last week of May to ensure that interactions had started between the mentees and mentors. As part of our follow-up we send mails to those who had not started interacting yet to make sure that they start interacting. We called these mentees once again in the first week of June. The final statistics of the number of students who have started interacting with their mentor is as follows,

Mentees contacted : 39

Mentees who were not reachable  : 9

Mentees who have started interacting : 20

Mentees who have not started interacting : 19

Towards the end of the program feedback mails were sent on 10th July to both mentees and mentors.

Challenges faced and improvements to be done:

  1. 1. Lots of mentees did not interact because they felt that they were already well settled in the city of their internship by the time they were allotted mentors and so it was difficult to convince them to interact with their mentors.
  2. 2. As the allotment was done manually this year, many people suggested that the allotment be made better i.e. to give more priority to sector in which the alumnus works rather than his/her department.These changes will be incorporated in the coming phase.
  3. 3. In this phase we were not able to include the foreign internship students which we aim to do in the next phase.
  4. 4. Depending on the feedback may think of extending the program in the winter-phase as well.

Achievements of the Program:

  1. 1. Through the program we have managed to take student-Alumni relations to a higher level .
  2. 2. With the launch of Mentor@intern we have managed to make the mentorship program a yearlong affair.
  3. 3. In the very first phase, we have had 20 successful interactions out of 48 with some of these interactions being so fruitful that some mentees have suggested that they would wish to continue their interactions even after the phase gets over.
  4. 4. Through this program we have managed to realize the biggest goal of SARC i.e. improving the network of students with the Alumni.
